**ROME IV Diagnostic Criteria for Nausea and Vomiting Disorders**

*Chronic Nausea Vomiting Syndrome (CNVS)*
- Bothersome nausea and/or one or more episodes of vomiting per week
- Exclusion of self-induced vomiting, eating disorders, regurgitation, or rumination
- No organic or metabolic disease likely to explain symptoms on routine investigations
- Criteria fulfilled for the last 3 months with symptom onset at least 6 months prior to diagnosis

*Cyclic Vomiting Syndrome (CVS)*
- Stereotypical episodes of vomiting: acute onset and lasting less than 1 week
- At least three discrete episodes in the prior year and two in the past 6 months, occurring at least 1 week apart
- No vomiting between episodes, although other milder symptoms possible
- Criteria fulfilled for the last 3 months with symptom onset at least 6 months prior to diagnosis
- A family history of migraines could support the diagnosis

*Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome (CHS)*
- Episodes of vomiting resembling CVS
- Prolonged cannabis use prior to symptom onset
- Relief of symptoms with sustained cessation of cannabis
- Criteria fulfilled for the last 3 months with symptom onset at least 6 months prior to diagnosis
- Frequent hot bathing or showering to relieve symptoms

*Rumination Syndrome*
- Persistent or recurrent regurgitation of recently ingested food with subsequent re-chewing, spitting, or swallowing
- Regurgitation is not preceded by retching
- Criteria fulfilled for the last 3 months with symptom onset at least 6 months prior to diagnosis

Source: ROME IV Diagnostic Criteria for Disorders of Gut-Brain Interaction (DGBI), 16 January 2016
