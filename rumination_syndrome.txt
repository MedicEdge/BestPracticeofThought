**Rumination Syndrome**

Diagnostic Criteria:
- Recurrent regurgitation of recently ingested food into the mouth with subsequent spitting or remastication and swallowing
- Non-painful and effortless regurgitation that is not preceded by retching
- Criteria must be fulfilled for the last 3 months with symptom onset at least 6 months prior to diagnosis

Supportive Criteria:
- Regurgitation is usually not preceded by nausea
- The regurgitant may be recognizable food with a potentially pleasant taste
- The regurgitation tends to stop when the regurgitated material becomes acidic

Source: ROME IV Diagnostic Criteria for DGBIs, 16 January 2016
